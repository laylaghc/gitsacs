Standard 13.7

Standard:

The institution ensures adequate physical facilities and resources, both on and off campus, that appropriately serve the needs of the institution's educational programs, support services, and other mission-related activities. (Physical resources)

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

Georgia Highlands College (GHC) has well-maintained and ample physical facilities and grounds to meet to the needs of its students, faculty, and staff. As a member of the University System of Georgia (USG), GHC adheres to the USG's governance and benefits from yearly state funding allocations. In addition to teaching and learning allocations, the State Legislature, Governor, and the Board of Regents (BOR) allocate significant funding annually for renovations, repairs, maintenance, and capital construction to ensure that all USG member institutions have appropriate physical facilities. GHC's facilities are more than suitable to accomplish its mission, as supported by the following description and documentation.

GHC Physical Locations

GHC's campus is located in Rome, GA. The college also has three instructional sites in Cartersville, Dallas, and Marietta. The Heritage Hall facility in downtown Rome that houses the Dental Hygiene and Nursing programs is considered part of the main campus. GHC maintains that each of its facilities are well equipped to help achieve the overall mission of educating students and supporting faculty and staff. GHC's various facilities are more than adequate to fulfill the mission of serving the students of Northwest Georgia. A description of the campus, along with visual evidence of GHC's upkeep of its campus and each instructional site is listed as follows.

Floyd Campus

GHC's website [1] states that "Floyd location was opened in 1970 and is located in the Six Mile Post area in Floyd County and is the original campus. As GHC's only campus, it has 15 buildings and includes a soccer field and tennis courts. It provides students with access to a 20-acre tract behind the campus that is a protected natural wetland ecosystem, which includes a 1,200-foot boardwalk and two observation platforms. The overall campus totals 251,975 gross square feet of space. Part of the Floyd Campus is a building named RESA. This is a leased space that houses the Northwest Regional Education Services Agency. The campus tour video [2] offers a unique perspective of the Floyd Campus for inquiring guest or prospective students."

Cartersville Instructional Site

Located in fast growing Bartow County, Georgia Highlands College's Cartersville Instructional site opened in 2005 with its main 130,787 square foot academic building. The Academic Building has 25 classrooms and associated spaces and 75 offices for faculty, staff, and administrators. In 2012, due to growth, the college added an additional PPV Student Center building, which added an additional 56,646 square feet of space. The student center provides a gym, fitness room, cafe, bookstore, and club spaces. The most recent addition, to the Cartersville Instructional Site is its STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and Math) building in 2018 giving the campus a square footage total of approximately 246,973 feet of space. The Cartersville website [3] states that in the "Spring semester of 2019, the Cartersville Site officially opened a new academic building that targeted our growing need for STEAM (science, technology, engineering, art, math) classrooms. With five science labs, six computer labs, thirteen general classrooms, and an art studio." The STEAM building added 59,540 gross square feet to the site's totals. It also includes a 56,646 square-foot student center. The student center's primary function is recreation. It includes a gym, fitness room, cafe, bookstore, and club spaces. The building has two offices and one associated classroom space. The Academic Building completed in 2005, has 25 classrooms and associated spaces and 75 offices for faculty, staff, and administrators. This video [4] provides a great perspective of the instructional site.

Marietta Instructional Site

GHC website states [5] that "the Marietta location was opened in 2005." Located in a growing Cobb County, and approximately 18 miles from downtown Atlanta, this location became GHC's third site, opening for classes on the campus of Southern Polytechnic State University (now Kennesaw State University.) In May of 2022, GHC's Marietta location expanded and relocated to 1090 Northchase Parkway, Suite 150. This site contains six lecture rooms, two computer classrooms a wet lab and Library learning commons. This new location, with a dedicated 21,000 square feet of space, allows GHC to better serve the students of Marietta, Fulton, Cobb and Cherokee counties. Expansion plans also include a Micro Mart, Faculty offices, Student Lounge, and additional parking. A site video [6] is included to provide a guided tour of Marietta. Please note that GHC leases this location and therefore does not renovate the building itself. Therefore, a listing of renovations is not included in this section.

Assessment of Facilities

GHC conducts regular surveys of students and employees. The Presently Attending Student Survey (PASS) assesses the student experience at the college. The Organizational Climate (Org Climate) survey seeks feedback from college employees about the state of the college. Both groups are asked about the overall maintenance of campus. Both groups rate the college facilitates highly.

Facility Management and Maintenance

GHC takes a collaborative approach to the management and maintenance of college facilitates. Each site has a campus dean or manager whose position description [9] requires them to work closely with the academic deans on classroom assignment and "identify maintenance concerns on campus/instructional site" to the facilitates team. GHC's Director of Plant Operations [10] manages the operations of the Plant team. The team includes a master electrician, building maintenance supervisors/technicians, shipping and receiving personnel, as well as contracted cleaning services. While the college does not have a facilities master plan, the Director of Plant Operations works with their supervisor (CBO and VP of Finance), campus deans, academic deans, and other appropriate administrators.

Security of Facilities

Each location is patrolled by POST certified security officers under the leadership of the college's Chief of Police [11]. The Floyd Campus and Cartersville instructional site have 24-hour security. Heritage Hall, Paulding instructional site and the Marietta instructional site are patrolled only during normal working hours. The college website states [12] "For your convenience and protection, Georgia Highlands College has professional Police Officers on duty during normal operating hours at each campus location. These officers provide physical security, fire protection and prevention, emergency first aid, safety programs and inspections, and investigate and enforce violations of the Code of Student Conduct and criminal offenses on GHC property."

Risk Management

GHC abides by the policies and processes as dictated in the State of Georgia's DOAS [13] concerning Risk Management, Insurance, surplus property disposal and Fleet Management.

Information Technology

Georgia Highlands Division of Information Technology ensures that IT service for the campus and instructional sites exceeds required parameters. According to the IT website [14], "the Division of Information Technology configures, installs, and maintains all information technology resources in use at Georgia Highlands College. This includes computing hardware, software applications, and network infrastructure as well as the technical support we provide to all students (including future students), faculty, and staff." Student Technology needs are fully met by an IT representative at each location. The IT Strategic Plan documents [15] the vision, mission and how the technology is implemented. Service requests are completed via a ticketing system [16]. This provides constant access to address problems from any location.

Parking

Students have adequate parking at each location, either owned by the college or leased. The Traffic Information website describes the process of registration, regulations, fines, and fees. Each lot has adequate spaces for individuals with accessibility needs.

Audits

GHC has had no findings related to fixed assets in an auditor's report on internal control or federal awards audit for the last ten years.

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the college now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
