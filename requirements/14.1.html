Standard 14.1

Standard:

The institution (a) accurately represents its accreditation status and publishes the name, address, and telephone number of SACSCOC in accordance with SACSCOC's requirements and federal policy; and (b) ensures all its branch campuses include the name of that institution and make it clear that their accreditation depends on the continued accreditation of the parent campus. (Publication of accreditation status)

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

Georgia Highlands College (GHC) accurately represents its accreditation status consistent with the expectations of the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on College (SACSCOC). GHC also understands that the accreditation of off campus instructional sites is dependent on the accreditation status of our main campus in Rome, Georgia. None of our other instructional sites meets the SACSCOC definition of a branch campus and are not represented as independent campuses. Therefore, Part B of this standard is not addressed.

GHC publishes its accreditation status in multiple locations, most notably in the course catalog [1] (page 4) and on the website under accreditation [2]. The statement is consistent with the "Representation of Status with SACSCOC" in the Commission's Policy Statement on "Institutional Obligations for Public Disclosure." As required by this policy, the statement includes an accurate representation of its SACSCOC accreditation status that includes the name, address, and telephone number of SACSCOC in accordance with SACSCOC requirements and federal policy.

The statement reads:

Georgia Highlands College is accredited by the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges (SACSCOC) to award associate and baccalaureate degrees. Georgia Highlands College also may offer credentials such as certificates and diplomas at approved degree levels. Questions about the accreditation of Georgia Highlands College may be directed in writing to the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges at 1866 Southern Lane, Decatur, GA 30033-4097, by calling (404) 679-4500, or by using information available on SACSCOC's website (www.sacscoc.org).

When accreditation is affirmed in other institutional publications, GHC seeks to represent it accurately. The institution is aware that SACSCOC accreditation is a mark of quality for the institution as a whole and does not imply specific program accreditation. Additionally, GHC avoids the phrase "fully accredited " since partial accreditation is not possible.

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the college now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
