Standard 12.1

Standard:

The institution provides appropriate academic and student support programs, services, and activities consistent with its mission. (Student support services)  [CR]

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

Georgia Highlands College (GHC) is a state college in the University System of Georgia (USG) and operates under the authority of the Board of Regents (BOR). As a state college, GHC provides appropriate academic and student support programs, services, and activities consistent with its mission.

GHC's Access Mission

The mission statement of GHC is as follows:

The mission of Georgia Highlands College, a state college of the University System of Georgia, is to provide access to excellent educational opportunities for the intellectual, cultural, and physical development of a diverse population through pre-baccalaureate associate degree transfer programs, career associate degree programs, and targeted baccalaureate degree programs that meet the economic development needs of the region [1].

In addition to the College's mission statement, the BOR and USG describe expectations of the mission of a state college. Specifically:

Institutions included in the balanced bachelor's and associate-level state colleges group offer bachelor's degrees, associate programs, and general education courses, but no graduate programs. These state colleges are characterized as balanced bachelor's and associate-level degrees with bachelor's programs focused on specialized academic and economic development program areas and regional, college-educated workforce needs. The Nexus degree can be offered at state colleges. The emphasis at these state colleges is on teaching and service with limited focus on basic or applied research activity (BOR 2.8 [2]).

Advising

Advising at GHC is the focus of the institution's QEP. As such, a fuller description of the services provided by advising can be found in the QEP Impact Report. As a college, GHC considers advising an essential activity to promote overall intellectual, cultural, and physical development. Both professional advisors and faculty members engage in advising and helping guide students in their educational journey at GHC, from start to finish, from organizing and running orientations to helping celebrate graduation. GHC defines advising as a process that occurs throughout the academic year, not just during designated course selection and registration periods.

Auxiliary Services

Auxiliary Services operates under the Division of Finance and Administration and includes the bookstore, campus dining, vending services, parking, and printing/copying services. While providing all these services, the Department focuses especially on keeping costs low for GHC students, thus contributing to the access component of the college's mission. The bookstore, for example, offers items such as textbooks, school supplies, spirit wear, and technology, all at competitive prices. Bookstore personnel also distribute the textbooks, free of charge, to dual enrollment students. A similar commitment to ensuring access and affordability is found across the Department's culture.

Campus Assessment, Response, and Evaluation (CARE) Team and Early Alert

Consistent with the mission of the college of promoting access to excellent educational opportunities and providing support for students who might struggle in the college, GHC operates a CARE team and Early Alert system to better support students. The process for both systems is noted on the College's website [3].

CARE Team. The GHC CARE Team responds to notices of students who are distressed or exhibit questionable behavior(s). Once a CARE ticket is received, it is promptly screened by the inter-departmental CARE Team. The response is based on the level of severity indicated on the ticket and interventions may be put into place to promote student and campus safety.

Early Alert. Via the Early Alert system, faculty can raise an alert to notify the student's advisor and appropriate support team regarding academic performance or a general concern. These support teams (tutoring, counseling, advising, etc.) reach out to the student and encourage participation in the noted support.

Center for Excellence in Teaching and Learning

The Center for Excellence in Teaching and Learning at Georgia Highlands College strongly contributes towards access to excellent educational opportunities and intellectual development at the college by providing research-based development for full- and part-time faculty who teach in any modality (in-person, hybrid, and online). CETL staff facilitate high-impact, comprehensive pedagogical and leadership development, encourage and recognize teaching excellence and innovation, curate and distribute resources to support professional and pedagogical growth, implement systematic and institutional strategic initiatives for student success, support preparation for annual institutional evaluations, and provide individual consultations for instructional strategies, technology, and wellbeing. CETL staff also administer the college's distance education program in conjunction with Information Technology (IT) staff and the Academic Schools.

Distance Education. Providing students with distance education is a joint venture between CETL, the Information Technology (IT) department, and the Academic Schools. CETL manages the institution's learning management system (D2L), provides tier 1 assistance to students and faculty and integrates faculty training on course development and design into New Faculty Academy. IT provides more advanced technical assistance, as well as ensures student and faculty credentials can access the system. The Academic Schools ensure quality assurance and regular substantive interaction in courses.

Financial Aid

GHC assists students with the costs associated with obtaining an education at the college through Financial Aid. The college offers federal and state grants, scholarships, and federal work-study opportunities to support tuition, fees, books, and other educational-related expenses. The financial aid program, including how to apply, types of aid, and eligibility requirements, is described in detail on the website [4], as well as in the catalog. Financial Aid also provides work opportunities through the Federal Work-Study program and other financial resources, such as a net price calculator [5]. Fitness Facilities

GHC encourages the physical development of students and faculty. On the Floyd Campus, a fitness lab, gymnasium, fitness trail, tennis courts, soccer field and other facilities are also available for student use. On the Cartersville site, a fitness lab, gymnasium, aerobics room, fitness trail and indoor running track are available for student use. At the Marietta and Paulding sites, GHC has a partnership with local fitness centers for student memberships.

Information Technology

The Division of Information Technology at GHC provides vital services to ensure GHC has access to excellent educational opportunities. IT provides computing hardware, software applications, and network infrastructure across all college locations. IT collaborates with the library and campus deans to provide students and faculty access to computer labs and laptops. IT also provides access and manages the student information system (Banner), Microsoft Office 365, Zoom, Respondus Lockdown Browser, and multifactor authentication (Duo Security). Students and faculty can seek assistance for any of these technology systems by submitting a ticket online [6] or emailing rt@highlands.edu.

Library

The GHC Library supports the college's mission of access to excellent educational opportunities and the intellectual and cultural development of GHC students and faculty. The library operates physical locations at all GHC's locations, providing a safe and welcoming space for all students. The department provides access to over 50,000 physical books housed across GHC's locations, as well as hundreds of thousands of eBooks, and millions of articles from magazines, journals, newspapers, and other sources online. As the college is a member of the University System of Georgia, patrons can access over 3.5 million books from all libraries in the University System and have them delivered to their preferred GHC location free of charge.

In addition to the collections, the GHC Library provides a range of instruction and programming geared towards meeting the intellectual and cultural needs of the GHC community. As faculty librarians, the library team teaches for-credit, one-shot, and embedded information literacy content. They also provide point-of-need instruction via reference transactions physically and virtually. The library also strives to provide meaningful and engaging programming. In recent years the library has developed new orientation workshops, de-stress midterms and finals events, and established a collegewide book club.

Male Minority Mentoring Initiative

Georgia Highlands African American and Minority Male Excellence (GHAME) is an academic success and leadership initiative for African American and Latino men at Georgia Highlands College. Brother 2 Brother (B2B) is a student organization that is part of GHAME. The initiative has won state and national recognition, and members have gone on to successful and meaningful careers around the country. Students are encouraged to participate in educational, social, and cultural activities organized by the GHAME Coordinator. Participants travel to conferences and workshops around the state and the region, as well as visit four-year colleges and universities. The program also hosts a student club that participates in Student Government Association programs and Student Activities events.

Student Engagement

Student Engagement offers leadership, social, cultural, co-curricular, and recreational opportunities that enhance students' educational experiences at all GHC locations, including virtually. Student Engagement oversees all campus clubs and organizational activities. Leadership opportunities for students are available through clubs, the Student Government Association, and the Student Leadership and Integrity Program. Examples of campus-wide student activities include the annual Week of Welcome, Fall Frenzy, Spring Fling, and campus career fairs. Student Engagement also supports the physical development of GHC students through recreational equipment checkout at the Floyd Campus. Students may borrow equipment such as sports balls, jump ropes, hula hoops, and golf clubs from the department.

Competitive Sports. Student Engagement at GHC further supports student physical development through competitive sports. This program offers two paths to students: league sports and intramural sports. League sports enable students to join competitive basketball, soccer, eSports, cheer, cross country, and volleyball teams. Teams play in either the National Intramural and Recreational Sports Association (NIRSA) league or an independent league with other University System of Georgia schools. Intramural sports enables GHC students to play basketball, tennis, billiards, ping-pong, bowling, volleyball, and dodge ball against other students at GHC. A full description of the GHC competitive sports program can be found on the website [7].

Student Government Association

The Student Government Association (SGA) is enshrined in the College's statutes as an essential voice for the students in the governance of the college. SGA consists of elected officers and representatives of all student clubs and organizations. An integrated program of student activities is provided through registered student organizations (RSO). Various RSOs have been established on campus for students with similar purposes and interests but may also include virtual meetings. RSOs at GHC include scholastic honor societies, academic departmental groups, student leadership, student media and special interest groups. Membership in all groups is open to all students. However, some RSOs may require additional criteria for joining as full members. A student at any campus is eligible to join any group, regardless of whether the organization is based at that student's primary campus.

Student Support Services

Student Support Services (SSS) provides a range of resources, including counseling support, disability access, and the food pantry. All services are offered from a student-centered perspective and free of charge. SSS has designed these services to assist students with making decisions and adjusting to college life, as well as provided resources designed to support student success. The SSS team is dedicated to serving all students at GHC.

Counseling Support. SSS provides counseling support to all GHC students. SSS offers mental health counseling that is designed to help students manage stress and anxiety effectively, clarify values and priorities, improve relationships, cope with grief and/or loss, explore issues of sexuality, overcome feelings of depression, resolve problems related to alcohol/drug use, and learn more effective coping strategies. Students can make an appointment on the department's website [8]. Counseling is offered at all GHC locations and virtually. Food Pantry. GHC works to combat food insecurity experienced by students with the Charger Food Pantry. The Charger Food Pantry is a free service available to any GHC student and is available at all GHC locations. The pantry is stocked with staple items like peanut butter, tuna, spaghetti sauce, non-perishable food items, and personal items. Students must check-in using their GHC ID at each visit and may collect up to 12 items once a week with no questions asked.

Testing Center

The Testing Center at GHC contributes to the access mission of the college by offering testing opportunities to students. At GHC, students who do not have ACT or SAT test scores or an acceptable GPA from high school are placed into learning support courses. The testing center offers the Accuplacer exam for students who wish to demonstrate their ability to succeed in college-level reading, writing, and math. Additionally, the Testing Center offers eLearning exams for midterms and finals, Independent Study Exams, eCore Exams, and TEAS exams. Testing is offered at all GHC locations and virtually, as appropriate.

Tutoring Center

The Tutoring Center at GHC provides a critical service to assist students in their intellectual development. As consistent with the access mission of the college, GHC students benefit from additional assistance in entry-level and gateway/foundational courses like Math and English. Therefore, the tutoring center offers drop-in Math and English assistance virtually in addition to in-person appointments. Students seeking assistance in other subjects, such as Science, are encouraged to make an appointment or meet with a tutor during their scheduled hours. An updated tutor schedule is posted semesterly on the tutoring center's website [9].

Veterans Affairs

GHC supports access for veterans and active-duty military, as well as their dependents, and spouses through the Veterans Affairs Office. The Veterans Affairs Coordinator provides guidance on obtaining, maintaining, and retaining educational benefits, as well as assisting students in certifying their enrollment to the VA for payments. The college also provides registration, which allows student-veterans to register for their classes.

Virtual Hub

Born out of necessity during the COVID-19 Pandemic, GHC developed and sustained a virtual hub as a convenient way for students to access services from anywhere. Students can video chat with customer service hosts, get answers to general college questions, or speak with specialists from Enrollment Management, Financial Aid, Admissions, Advising, Business Office, and Student Support Services, and others.

Writing Center

The GHC Writing Center supports student access and intellectual development through a variety of services that assist students with navigating their writing process in a more effective way. The Center offers direct assistance with brainstorming, outlining, grammar, revisions, and research. It is staffed by GHC's English Faculty members and is operated under the School of Humanities. Students can receive assistance face-to-face at the Floyd Campus and the Cartersville instructional site. Online support is also available.

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the college now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
