Standard 10.2

Standard:

The institution makes available to students and the public current academic calendars, grading policies, cost of attendance, and refund policies. (Public information)

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

Georgia Highlands College (GHC) makes available to students and the public the current academic calendars, grading policies, cost of attendance, and the refund policies on its website. These policies apply to all campus and instructional sites.

Academic Calendars

GHC publishes academic calendars on its website [1]. This includes the current academic year, as well as archived academic calendars beginning with the 2015-2016 academic year. The current academic calendar is also integrated into the GHC Master Calendar [2].

Grading Policies

Grading policies are published on the college's website in the Academic Information section of the GHC catalog [3] (page 41). These policies describe the College's grading system, how students access grade reports, the computation of grade point averages, repeating courses, the academic appeals procedure, academic progress, and alternative credit. Students receive specific information about Evaluations and Assessments, as well as the Grading Scale for each class via the course syllabi [4] instructors provide on the first day of class.

Cost of Attendance

Cost of attendance information includes tuition and fees. It also includes in-state and out-of-state tuition rates and a cost comparison guide with several other colleges and universities in northwest Georgia. This information is published on the college website [5] and is available to both students and the public. Information pertaining to paying for classes, account balances, and payment plans is found with the Bursar's Office on the college website [6]. Likewise, the Office of Financial Aid provides information about financial aid and scholarships on the college website [7].

Refund Policy

GHC publishes refund information in two places on the college website. First, the Bursar's Office gives detailed refund information [8]. Next, the GHC catalog [3] describes fee refund policy under the section entitled Tuition and Fees (page 37).

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the college now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
