Standard 10.6

Standard:

An institution that offers distance or correspondence education:
  1. ensures that the student who registers in a distance or correspondence education course or program is the same student who participates in and completes the course or program and receives the credit.
  2. has a written procedure for protecting the privacy of students enrolled in distance and correspondence education courses or programs.
  3. ensures that students are notified in writing at the time of registration or enrollment of any projected additional student charges associated with verification of student identity.
(Distance and correspondence education)

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

Georgia Highlands College (GHC) ensures that the student who registers in a distance education course is the same student who completes and participates in the course or program and receives credit; has a written procedure for protecting the privacy of student enrolled in distance education courses and programs; and ensures that students are notified in writing at the time of registration or enrollment of any projected additional student charges associated with verification of student identity.

Student Identities

During the GHC application process, applicants electronically sign a legally binding statement confirming everything they submitted on the application is true in accordance with O.C.G.A. 16-10-71 [1]. Each applicant is assigned a unique GHC student identification number (GHC ID). A Single Sign-on (SSO) account is created and linked to that GHC ID. New students may review their GHC ID and initial SSO credentials by visiting the GHC Banner Portal web page and clicking GHC ID Lookup link. Students receive an instructions on looking up their GHC ID and SSO information via email and in the New Student Orientation Packet on page 18 [2].

GHC ensures the integrity of all student SSO accounts with multifactor authentication (MFA) in compliance with the University System of Georgia (USG) IT Handbook 3.1.2 Managing Multifactor Authentication (USG IT, p. 33 [3]). Students are required to register a mobile device or tablet and verify their identity through multiple factors during each authentication that includes:

  • Something they know - entering their password; and
  • Something they have - verification of a code or push-notification through their mobile device or tablet.

Consistent with SACSCOC's Policy statement on distance education and correspondence courses, GHC employs several technologies to ensure the student who registers is the one completing the coursework and receiving credit. Primary systems in place include Single Sign-on (SSO) and multifactor authentication (MFA). The college also provides remote proctoring via Responds Lockdown Browser, video conferencing via Zoom and Microsoft Teams, and remote testing applications (Exemplify).

All GHC students are bound by the Acceptable Use Policy [4] and Acceptable Use Standard [5] which details appropriate and ethical use of user accounts and GHC systems.

Student Privacy

The GHC Privacy Policy [6] details student rights granted by the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA) and also details that Office of the Registrar maintains and controls access to the official academic transcript and a "personal folder" containing official documents for each student [7].

GHC, in accordance with the USG Business Procedures Manual section 12.6.4 [8], follows the best practice of limiting potential exposure by utilizing the minimum data needed for processes that involve privacy protected data.

Additionally, through data subject request procedures, GHC:

  • Implements mechanisms for communicating data processing purposes, practices, associated privacy risks and options for enabling student's preferences and requests where allowable by law.
  • Maintains records of unintended data disclosures to be accessed for investigative review.
  • Implements policies and processes for receiving, tracking, and responding to complaints, concerns and questions from students about organizational privacy practices.
  • Communicates requested data corrections or deletions to students making such requests.
  • Notifies impacted students and/or organizations as required by law concerning a privacy breach or event; and,
  • Provides students with mitigation mechanisms as required by law to address impacts of a privacy breach or event.

Student Fees

GHC does not currently charge any additional fees for distance education students associated with verification of student identity. Prior to summer 2020, the distance learning fee was $25 per web-based course. This was phased out over a three-year period (2018 – 2020). GHC did not charge the distance learning fee in Summer of 2020 based on the fee waivers that the USG implemented across the system for that semester due to COVID. The lack of a fee is shared with students on the tuition and fee schedule [9].

If any other proctoring fees were to be charged at any point in the future, they would be noted in the course description students see to select courses for registration each term. Input of the course descriptions is done by each School at GHC in coordination with the faculty teaching the course in question.

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the college now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
