Standard 14.4

Standard:

The institution (a) represents itself accurately to all U.S. Department of Education recognized accrediting agencies with which it holds accreditation and (b) informs those agencies of any change of accreditation status, including the imposition of public sanctions. (See SACSCOC policy "Accrediting Decisions of Other Agencies.")
(Representation to other agencies)

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

Georgia Highlands College (GHC) accurately represents itself to all U.S. Department of Education (USDOE) accrediting agencies with which it holds accreditation. Currently GHC holds accreditation with the Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges (SACSCOC), Accreditation Commission for Education in Nursing (ACEN) for the ASN and BSN programs, and Commission on Dental Accreditation (CODA). GHC has not yet experienced any change in its accreditation status which would require notification as required by 14.4.

Accurate Representation

SACSCOC, ACEN, and CODA are the only USDOE recognized accrediting agencies with which GHC holds accreditation (Office of Post-Secondary Education Database [1]). The most recent self-studies submitted to each accrediting agency reveal that GHC has described itself accurately to both of those accreditors and that information is consistent across accreditors.

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the college now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
