Standard 9.2

Standard:

The institution offers one or more degree programs based on at least 60 semester credit hours or the equivalent at the associate level; at least 120 semester credit hours or the equivalent at the baccalaureate level; or at least 30 semester credit hours or the equivalent at the post-baccalaureate, graduate, or professional level. The institution provides an explanation of equivalencies when using units other than semester credit hours. The institution provides an appropriate justification for all degree programs and combined degree programs that include fewer than the required number of semester credit hours or its equivalent unit. (Program length)  [CR]

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

Georgia Highlands College (GHC) only uses the semester credit hour system to measure course completion toward degree completion. GHC offers 3 career associate programs, 2 transfer associate programs, and 1 nexus degree program, which require at least 60 semester credit hours. GHC also offers 8 baccalaureate degree programs, which require at least 120 semester credit hours. GHC does not offer any post-baccalaureate degree, graduate, or professional level programs, nor any combined degree programs or direct assessment competency-based programs. The college does offer learning support courses, which are numbered less than 1000 and not considered to be college-level. Learning support courses are therefore excluded from the semester credit hour requirements.

Policies of GHC's Governing Board for Degree Program Length

GHC is a member of the University System of Georgia (USG). The USG is a statewide governing entity for Georgia and operates under the authority of the Board of Regents (BOR). BOR and USG establish policies, which each member institution follows. These policies include the minimum number of credit hours for each degree program (BOR 3.8 [1]) and a definition of a credit hour being 750 minutes of instruction or equivalent (BOR 3.4.4 [2]). Per USG policy, career and transfer associate degrees, as well as nexus degrees must contain 60 semester credit hours. Bachelor's degrees must contain 120 semester credit hours.

Approval Process for GHC's Degree Programs

Degree programs at Georgia Highlands College are first approved at the institutional level via a curriculum committee (Faculty Senate Bylaws, Article VIII, Section 2, Subsection B, page 18 [3]). Upon approval, the President of the College and the Chancellor of the USG recommend changes and refer them to the Board of Regents for approval (BOR 3.6.1 [4]). As illustrated by a fully accepted program proposal [5], this process enshrines a commitment to academic rigor, quality, and program length.

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the college now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
