Standard 10.3

Standard:

The institution ensures the availability of archived official catalogs (digital or print) with relevant information for course and degree requirements sufficient to serve former and returning students. (Archived information)

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

GHC is an institution in the University System of Georgia (USG) and operates under the authority of the Board of Regents (BOR). The BOR has a records retention policy (BOR Catalog/Bulletin Retention [1]), which requires GHC to maintain a permanent collection of "published catalogs/bulletins, schedule of classes booklets, and handbooks." Additionally, the BOR requires each of these catalogs to contain such information as course and degree requirements (BOR 2.2 [2]).

Location of Archived Catalogs

GHC's Library Archives maintains a digital collection of catalogs beginning with the 2002-2003 academic year. This digital collection of catalogs runs through the current academic year and is published online on the College's website [3].

Paper catalogs prior to the 2002-2003 academic year are stored in the GHC Library Archives. The GHC Library Archives Office has a full collection of paper catalogs that begins with the College's first catalog, namely the 1970-1972 catalog. The GHC Library Archives Office has a partial digitized collection [4] of these catalogs. Catalogs are currently being added to this digitized collection as they are scanned. Members of the public who wish to view paper copies of catalogs beginning with the 1970-1972 edition, including editions that are not currently digitized, may visit the GHC Library Archives Office. It is located in the GHC Library on the Floyd campus. Members of the public may also contact the GHC Library Archives Office at archives@highlands.edu.

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the College now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
