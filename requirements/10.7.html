Standard 10.7

Standard:

The institution publishes and implements policies for determining the amount and level of credit awarded for its courses, regardless of format or mode of delivery. These policies require oversight by persons academically qualified to make the necessary judgments. In educational programs not based on credit hours (e.g., direct assessment programs), the institution has a sound means for determining credit equivalencies. (Policies for awarding credit)

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

Georgia Highlands College (GHC) has published and implemented policies for determining the amount and level of credit awarded for its courses, regardless of format or mode of delivery. These policies are created by University System of Georgia (USG) and Board of Regents (BOR), under which the College operates. The College does not offer any direct assessment programs.

Credit Hour Policies and Practices Applicable To GHC

GHC relies upon the definition of a semester and credit hour as presented in the policy manual of its governing body, the USG and BOR (BOR 3.4 [1]). The Faculty Senate Bylaws [2] detail the procedures in which the College approves courses and verifies the number of semester hour credits awarded. These policies and procedures offer a concise collection of requirements that addresses this standard and reflect the ways in which the institution operates.

Academic Calendar. BOR policy 3.4.2 [3] is the Uniform Academic Calendar policy and is published on the USG website. This policy requires each USG institution to publish an academic calendar that "consist[s] of two semesters, each with at least 15 weeks of instructional time, as defined by federal regulations issued by the United States Department of Education. The 15 weeks of instructional time shall not include registration or final examinations." GHC publishes the academic calendar [4] on the College's website [5] and in the course catalog [6] (page 11). Of note, GHC offers courses that vary in length between 15 weeks and 8 weeks, as well as a Maymester.

Instruction Time. BOR policy 3.4.4 [7] is the Instruction Time policy and is published on the USG website. This policy requires a "minimum of 750 minutes of instruction or equivalent … for each semester credit hour" and that "courses … fewer than 15 instruction weeks are also required to contain the same total number of hours as the same course offered in a 15-week semester format." Of note, the BOR policy only addresses seat-time. This is not an indication of the time the student must spend to successfully complete the semester credit hour at GHC. In practice, GHC's expectation is that students must spend at least two hours outside of class for each hour in class or the equivalent student engagement time per credit hour. For example, in traditional lecture courses out-of-class time is required to prepare for and to carry out the assignments associated with the instructional contact time for the credit hours awarded.

Courses are sometimes offered via traditional face-to-face, hybrid, and online delivery modalities. Online delivery methods include synchronous and asynchronous formats using the Learning Management System, Desire2Learn (D2L). Although online courses may deliver their instructional time and assignments in entirely different ways from their face-to-face counterpart, both courses are expected to achieve comparable content coverage, student learning outcomes, and student engagement time and effort.

This is consistent with federal guidelines and the SACSCOC's Credit Hours Policy Statement [8], which defines a credit hour as "an amount of work represented in intended learning outcomes and verified by evidence of student achievement [emphasis added] that is an institutionally established equivalency that reasonably approximates not less than one hour of classroom or direct faculty instruction and a minimum of two hours out of class student work each week for approximately fifteen weeks for one semester." Instructional time is adjusted based on the calendar applied to the course. The following table is the expected student class time per three credit hour course for traditional face-to-face courses.

Academically Qualified Persons

GHC's Curriculum Committee ensures quality and consistency across the College's curricular offerings and programs. The Faculty Senate Bylaws [2] enshrines the College's curriculum committee and is published on the College's website. The committee is an interdisciplinary team, which reviews curriculum proposals directly from the academic schools. Their preview includes providing recommendations on changes to existing courses; proposals for new courses; proposals for new programs; changes to existing programs; course prefixes, numbers, titles, credit hours; and adjustments in credit hours for courses.

The committee consists of the Provost, who shall serve as Chair, and one faculty member from each academic school, including the library. The Provost, a representative for Learning Support, a representative for the General Education Council (when applicable), and Registrar shall serve as ex-officio members. The committee receives and recommends the requirements for all degree programs and courses, which includes semester credit hours, to the entire faculty for consideration. An example of this committee can be evidenced by the committee's minutes [9]. This group and process constitutes "persons academically qualified to make the necessary judgments," as required by this standard.

Adherence to Policy in Academic Year Semester Scheduling

GHC adheres to the Uniform Academic Calendar policy (BOR 3.4.2 [3]) and Instruction Time Policy (BOR 3.4.4 [7]). Each academic calendar consists of two semesters with at least 15 weeks of instructional time, excluding registration and final exams, as defined by federal regulations issued by the United States Department of Education.

Evidence of compliance with the 15-week semester policy can be seen in GHC's Academic Calendars for Fall Semester 2022 and Spring Semester 2023 [4]. The calendars state:

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the College now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
