Standard 10.9

Standard:

The institution ensures the quality and integrity of the work recorded when an institution transcripts courses or credits as its own when offered through a cooperative academic arrangement. The institution maintains formal agreements between the parties involved, and the institution regularly evaluates such agreements. (Cooperative academic arrangements)

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

Georgia Highlands College (GHC) is a member of the University System of Georgia (USG) under the governance of the Board of Regents (BOR). GHC has cooperative academic arrangements for general degree credit and specific degree programs. These agreements allow GHC to transcript courses and credits for learning that has occurred through the cooperative program. GHC only undertakes cooperative academic arrangements that align with the mission of providing access to excellent educational opportunities for the intellectual, cultural and physical development of a diverse population; the purpose of providing access to a teaching and learning environment that prepares students to thrive in a global society; and the shared value of providing access to high quality, affordable educational opportunities within the diverse communities the College serves (GHC Mission and Goals [1]). 

Cooperative Academic Agreements

The college undertakes cooperative academic agreements to expand access to credentialed expertise and resources that would not be available to GHC students otherwise. The partnerships allow GHC to utilize courses that Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges (SACSCOC) accredited sister institutions in the USG have developed. Generally, there are of two types of agreements in place: those that allow for general credit toward any degree and those that are targeted toward specific degree programs.

General Degree Credit

GHC maintains two partnerships that allow students to take courses outside of GHC that the College transcripts as its own for credit toward general education requirements or special topics in any of the degree programs. These partnerships exist between GHC and sister USG institutions as part of the USG's eCore Initiative and the USG Goes Global Initiative. These agreements are reviewed, evaluated, and approved annually.

eCore. eCore (electronic core-curriculum) courses are delivered through a consortium agreement between GHC and affiliated sister colleges and universities in the USG. The academic integrity of courses in the agreement are under the review, approval, and control of GHC's internal curriculum governance processes in addition to the shared oversight of eCore and the USG Regents Advisory Committee (RAC) on eCore. Course credits earned by completing eCore courses are awarded by GHC and are transferable within the USG as well as other regionally accredited institutions.

eCore courses are designed, developed, taught, and supported by faculty and staff who are employed at SACSCOC accredited colleges and universities within the USG. Verification of the appropriate credentials for a faculty member to teach a specific course first comes from eCore and then is approved by GHC (eCore Letter of Commitment [4]).

The core curriculum for eCore primarily consists of courses required during the first two years of college and are generally applicable in any degree path. All eCore courses are taught entirely online, except for the occasional proctored exam. While not all the core curriculum classes are available via eCore, the remaining core classes needed by a student can be taken through traditional means at GHC (USG eCore Site [5]).

Regular evaluation of the eCore agreement among the USG partner institutions, including GHC, occurs through the ongoing work of the eCore RAC. Direct evaluation of GHC's involvement with eCore is reviewed at annual eCore review meetings hosted at GHC (GHC eCore Partnership Report [6]).

USG Goes Global. USG Goes Global (USGG) is a collaborative curriculum development and enrollment opportunity for students in approved study abroad courses. USGG Study abroad courses are made available to schools across the USG from their originating college or university through the cooperative academic arrangement.

USGG is an administrative unit of the USG that partners with USG institutions to identify quality, affordable, and impactful faculty-led study abroad experiences which are open to all USG undergraduate students. USGG provides a mix of core and special topics courses in international locations and assists in system-wide marketing efforts to provide a solid base of enrollment in the program (USG Goes Global Info [7], USG Goes Global Programs [8]).

The academic integrity of courses in the agreement are under the review, approval, and control of the governance processes at GHC in conjunction with an arrangement with the USG Curriculum Leadership Committee noted in the agreement itself. All course credit is awarded by GHC and is transferable within the USG as well as other regionally accredited institutions. Through the regular evaluation of the USGG agreement and the ongoing work of the USG Curriculum Leadership Committee in which GHC participates, GHC ensures the academic quality of all work resulting in credit being recorded on a student's transcript.

Specific Degree Programs. To offer courses that complete degree programs that the college might not otherwise be able to offer, GHC undertakes cooperative academic agreements with USG sister institutions. The USG facilitates the establishment of the MOUs with all parties involved. Currently GHC has five such MOUs which outline requirements for the review and approval of course content and faculty qualifications, the evaluation of the quality and integrity of course content and student learning outcomes, continuous improvement, and assurance of academic quality. These facets are reviewed regularly among all consortium members and are checked for congruity with GHC's mission. Each MOU establishes the responsibilities of each partner and establishes the members of a curriculum leadership committee for each program that maintains governance over the programs. GHC has a defined member on each curriculum leadership committee.

    Specifically, each MOU contains the following elements:

    • Responsibilities of the partner institutions
      • Review and approval of course content
      • Review and approval of faculty qualifications
      • Regularly scheduled evaluation of the quality and integrity of the course content and student learning outcomes
      • Continuous improvement
      • Assurance of academic quality
      • Regular review of the agreement
    • Responsibilities of a common cooperative administrative support services
      • Maintain documentation of the partnership
      • Coordinate curriculum development and delivery
      • Administratively coordinate teaching assignments
      • Coordinate assessments
      • Support services
      • Governance through curriculum leadership committees and the assignment of a specific GHC administrator and faculty member to that role
    • Appendix outlining the courses required of each MOU that incorporates a full degree or general courses that can be utilized by GHC for its own degree programs
    • Listing of all policies and procedures that are outside of the MOU and assignment of responsibilities for enactment of each including:
      • Advising
      • Academic calendar
      • Access and accommodations
      • Accreditation oversight and policy compliance
      • Admission
      • Assessment
      • Communication
      • Course approval
      • Course curriculum
      • Course evaluation
      • Course scheduling
      • Course withdrawal
      • Discrimination and harassment
      • Evaluation of instruction
      • Faculty credentials and teaching assignments
      • Financial aid
      • Institutional fees
      • Library services
      • Record retention
      • Registration
      • Student complaints
      • Technology services
      • Tuition

Criminal Justice Baccalaureate Degree. GHC has entered an MOU with six other USG institutions to provide collaborative curriculum development and enrollment opportunities exclusively for students enrolled in approved Criminal Justice courses. Specific coursework will be delivered through a cooperative academic arrangement known as the Criminal Justice BS Cooperative Academic Arrangement. All institutions involved in the agreement are accredited by SACSCOC and adhere to the Principles of Accreditation Standard 10.9 on Cooperative Academic Arrangements and the Substantive Change Policy and Procedures pertaining to Cooperative Academic Arrangements.

The Criminal Justice degree offers a complete curriculum administratively coordinated by USG's eMajor program. eMajor degrees are offered fully online through SACSCOC-accredited institutions of the USG as a cooperative academic arrangement with functional support provided by USG eCampus. eMajor programs bring together the power and academic expertise of multiple USG institutions to provide online degrees that prepare students for workforce demands in Georgia and beyond (USG eMajor site [14]). Results of this program are reviewed regularly (GHC eMajor Criminal Justice Partnership Report [15]).

Georgia Film Academy (GFA). GHC has entered an MOU with twenty-two other USG institutions and two Technical College System of Georgia colleges to provide collaborative curriculum development and an enrollment opportunity exclusively for students enrolled in approved film, television, and digital entertainment courses. Specific coursework will be delivered through a cooperative academic arrangement known as the Georgia Film Consortium (GFC). All institutions involved in the agreement are accredited by SACSCOC and adhere to the Principles of Accreditation Standard 10.9 on Cooperative Academic Arrangements and the Substantive Change Policy and Procedures pertaining to Cooperative Academic Arrangements.

The GFA MOU does not offer a complete degree program to GHC students. Instead, the MOU allows GHC to access courses that we can then use in degree offerings. Currently, these GFA courses are utilized in GHC's Associate of Arts degree with a Film Production pathway (GHC Film Production Pathway [16]). Results of this agreement are reviewed regularly (GFA Advisory Council Minutes [17], GFA Enrollment Report [18]).

FinTech Academy (FTA). GHC has entered an MOU with twenty-two other USG institutions to provide collaborative curriculum development and an enrollment opportunity exclusively for students enrolled in approved financial technology (FinTech) courses to be delivered through a cooperative academic arrangement to be known as the FinTech Cooperative Academic Arrangement. All institutions involved in the agreement are accredited by SACSCOC and adhere to the Principles of Accreditation Standard 10.9 on Cooperative Academic Arrangements and the Substantive Change Policy and Procedures pertaining to Cooperative Academic Arrangements. 

The FTA MOU does not offer a complete degree program to GHC students but instead allows GHC to access courses that are utilized in degree offerings. Currently, these FTA courses are utilized in the Nexus degree in Supply Chain in Financial Technology and as elective credit in our BBA program (GHC Nexus Degree [19], GHC BBA in Healthcare Management [20], GHC BBA in Logistics and Supply Chain Management [21]).

Financial Technology Associate Degree. GHC has entered an MOU with three other USG institutions to provide collaborative curriculum development and an enrollment opportunity exclusively for students enrolled in approved financial technology (FinTech) courses to be delivered through a cooperative academic arrangement to be known as the FinTech AS (Associate of Science) eMajor Cooperative Academic Arrangement. All institutions involved in the agreement are accredited by SACSCOC and adhere to the Principles of Accreditation Standard 10.9 on Cooperative Academic Arrangements and the Substantive Change Policy and Procedures pertaining to Cooperative Academic Arrangements.

The FTA MOU does not offer a complete degree program to GHC students but instead allows GHC to access courses that we can then use in degree offerings. Currently, these FTA courses are utilized in GHC's Associate of Science degree/FinTech pathway (GHC FinTech Pathway Sheet [22]).

RN to BSN. GHC has entered an MOU with thirteen other USG institutions to provide collaborative curriculum development and an enrollment opportunity exclusively for students enrolled in approved nursing courses. Specific coursework will be delivered through a cooperative academic arrangement known as the RN-BSN common courses. All institutions involved in the agreement are accredited by SACSCOC and adhere to the Principles of Accreditation Standard 10.9 on Cooperative Academic Arrangements and the Substantive Change Policy and Procedures pertaining to Cooperative Academic Arrangements.

These RN-BSN common courses are used as part of GHC's RN to BSN degree completion program (GHC website RN to BSN [23]). The results of this common course offering are reviewed regularly (RN BSN Collaboration Report [24]).

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the college now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
