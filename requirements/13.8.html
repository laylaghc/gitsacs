Standard 13.8

Standard:

The institution takes reasonable steps to provide a healthy, safe, and secure environment for all members of the campus community. (Institutional environment)

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

According to the Georgia Highlands College (GHC) Strategic Plan [1] Priority 1G, the college will "ensure campus facilities support safe and secure experiences for all. GHC also supports maintaining a healthy, safe, and secure environment, in adherence to its overall mission." The institution strives to provide a safe environment and facility infrastructure. The College adheres to all applicable rules, regulations, legislation, and guidance as supported through the Board of Regents (BOR) Policies and Procedures Manual (Campus Affairs, Section 6 [2]); GHC Policies and Procedures Manual [3]; and College Emergency Management Plan [4]. The institution has an ethical responsibility to take reasonable steps to provide a healthy, safe, and secure environment for all campus constituents. A healthy, safe, and secure environment enhances the accomplishment of its mission and contributes to more effective risk management.

Responsibility for Protecting the Institutional Environment

As CEO of the institution, the college's primary administrative responsibility for safety and security process, including college infrastructure and facilities, rests within the purview of the President. The organizational chart [5] for GHC reflects the relationship between the president and all associates delegated with these duties. This includes the Vice President of Finance and Administration, Chief of Police, and Director of Facilities. The President also maintains a cabinet, known as the President's Cabinet [6] which is responsible for the approvals of policies and procedures governing the management, operations, and strategic planning of the College. Policies and procedures adopted by the Cabinet shall be maintained in the Policy and Procedure Manual [3]. Ultimately, the safety and security of the college rests with each faculty and staff member at GHC.

Emergency Management Plan

GHC's Emergency Management Plan [4] is contained in the Department of Campus Safety's annual report. The report "applies to all personnel, facilities, grounds, and property owned and/or controlled by GHC including properties leased by the institution." During any declaration of a campus emergency, Campus Safety "shall place into immediate effect the appropriate procedures necessary in order to meet the emergency, safeguard persons and property, and maintain educational facilities. The Campus Safety office shall immediately consult with the president regarding the emergency and the possible need for a declaration of a campus state of emergency." 

Emergency Response Notifications

Georgia Highlands College maintains an Emergency Communications Plan [7] where the Marketing and Communications Office bears the primary responsibility to safeguard the college's public image and ensure a proper mechanism for campus safety and security in an emergency event. The ECP primary purpose is to outline the proper processes, procedures and policies relating to emergency events. The plan details Emergency Operations Centers (EOC) for each location, emphasizes responsibilities to each administrator as well as detailing communications and coordination protocols with local Police and Sheriff's department. GHC believes key to addressing a crisis is timely communication with internal constituents such as faculty, staff and students, as well as with the media and the public. Therefore, as soon as a crisis as identified:

  • The president and the college relations officer should be notified of any emergency, regardless of the time or day.
  • The magnitude and possible consequences of the crisis will be assessed by the president, college relations officer and safety personnel.
  • The president or the college relations officer will be the sole spokesman.
  • The vice presidents and campus dean(s) will then be notified and apprised of the situation.

GHC also uses the Notify System [8] as the fastest form of warning concerning emergency notifications. During class registration, students are automatically enrolled in this system and faculty/staff are encouraged to voluntarily opt-in to the notification system. The President can determine college wide delays, closures, or other safety related events through the Notify System. Additional notifications are also broadcast primarily through WXIA 11 ALIVE (Atlanta) news station. GHC internal email is used to deliver weather notifications and other related data as information to severe weather events.

The most recent example of testing the Emergency Communications Plan occurred in July 2022. A bomb threat was called initially specifying the Marietta Instructional Site as the location of peril. The 911 call came to Marietta City Police Department, which dispatched officers to facilitate evacuations from the site. GHC Police were contacted and GHC leadership were notified. In accordance with the ECP, the President closed the college while Marketing and Communications dispatched communications to the institution through GHC Alert/Inform, email, and a notification banner on the college website. Marketing and Communications also dispatched closure notifications to local media outlets. 

Facilities and Grounds

Facilities and grounds are regularly reviewed and tested for health and safety concerns through routine inspection by campus safety and the Department of Facilities. In order to comply with state safety and security concerns, the Director of the Physical Plant oversees the maintenance of operations of each campus and instructional sites. The department's stated mission [9] is to "maintain and enhance buildings, grounds and the physical infrastructure in a cost-effective, safe and environmentally responsible manner."

To optimally ensure the safety and security of all GHC facilities, each is inspected to meet local statutes, procedures, mandates, and regulations. In the process of safety and maintenance, the department uses a variety of measures. Their mission also states that facilities will "work closely with all other departments to provide the physical environment conducive to the highest excellence in teaching and learning." To facilitate this mission, the department maintains requests are submitted and tracked via the QWARE system and assures the security of offices and buildings via a Key Request system form and Alarm Code Request form. The office of Campus Safety and Physical Plant periodically collaborates to provide scheduled awareness training to educate faculty, staff, and students on the process for reporting physical and environmental health issues. Inspection reports can be found in the Annual Security Report and the Fire Safety Inspection [10].

Office of Campus Safety

The Office of Campus Safety at GHC is led by the Chief of Police who has overall authority and oversight concerning primary law enforcement for each of GHC's campus and instructional sites. Each GHC campus or instructional site has at least one certified Police Officer and the department is a state law enforcement agency. These police officers are on duty during college normal operational hours and for extended college related events. As required by the State of Georgia through the Department of Administrative Services (DOAS), GHC partners with Dynamic Security Inc. [11] to provide additional safety and security for our campus and instructional sites. GHC currently has five full time certified police officers.

GHC is required to report all campus crimes according to the Cleary Act [12]. This act mandates reporting by the federal government to ensure that crime statistics are available to the public. Campus Safety maintains a mandatory three-year report concerning selected crime statistics. These statistics are collected from each campus and instructional site; and with cooperation from any law enforcement officials or agencies connected with student safety and security. Data from the report can be found on GHC's website, under Campus Police, Cleary/Crime reports and specifically titled "GHC's Annual Security Report". There are also the Daily Police Crime Log [13], Georgia's Sex Offender Registry [14], and a Silent Witness platform [15].

Evacuation processes are outlined in the annual GHC Campus Safety Report [10]. 

Enforcement

In pursuit of establishing a culture that provides a safe, healthy and secure environment for all its members, Georgia Highlands College has a professional Police force [16] and Officers on duty during normal operating hours at each campus location. The Campus Safety website states that "these officers provide physical security, fire protection and prevention, emergency first aid, safety programs and inspections, and investigate and enforce violations of the Code of Student Conduct and criminal offenses on GHC property."

GHC Campus Police work closely [17] with "local, state and federal law enforcement agencies in order to provide for professional security, safety and emergency management services to the properties under its control." This includes GHC campus in Rome and instructional sites in Cartersville, Dallas and Marietta. In 2019 the college installed a 24/7 dispatch center to help with on and off hours emergencies at all sites. The college also follows state law as it concerns the development of Campus Carry [18].

Relationship with Local Law Enforcement

In order to maintain positive relationships with surrounding law enforcement agencies, and in collaboration with local law enforcement, according to the Emergency Management Plan [4], "the Campus Safety Department has partnerships and agreements with the municipalities of Rome, Dallas, Cobb County, Marietta, The Floyd County Police, the Bartow County Sheriff's Department, and other local law enforcement agencies on criminal investigations. The department's website states that "the Georgia Highlands College Campus Safety Department will provide and seek assistance from other federal, state, county, and local law enforcement agencies as needed."

Accommodations and Disability Access

The office of Student Support Services provides a variety of support to GHC's student population. Each campus and instructional site has access to these services either virtually or face to face. The website reads that "to help facilitate progression through college, Student Support Services provides resources from a student-centered perspective, free of charge, through personal counseling and disability-access." These services include counseling support, faculty, staff and student resources, disability access, outreach, and pantry for our food insecure populations. For 24/7 access to services, GHC partners with Christie Campus Health [19] to provide counseling support through the program named Thrive@GHC [20].  

Care Team

As a demonstrated method of promoting a safe and secure environment at GHC, the Department of Academic Affairs and Counseling and Career Services have implemented a CARE team [21] reporting system. The Care team is a process for faculty, staff and administrators to report incidents of student behavior that is displayed as dangerous to self or others. The Care team meets as needed to address concerns that may affect a student's academic welfare, classroom instruction or safety.  

Office of Student Engagement

The Office of Student Engagement provides a pathway to ensure students receive fair and equitable treatment with concerns of Student Conduct [22]. The website states that "this policy establishes the Code of Conduct for students and student organizations ensuring due process, which also affords fairness and equity in all student conduct investigations." The website provides an Incident Reporting Form [23] to document Code of Conduct violations.  

Wellness

Georgia Highlands College, in cooperation with the University System of Georgia's Well Being Program [24] and Virgin Pulse [25], offers a variety of wellness opportunities for employees. Also, for benefited employees, Human Resources also maintains partnership with local establishments offering flu shots, health screenings and other well-being services. Other health and wellness medical benefits are sponsored through the University System of Georgia's benefits plans [26]. The HR office also schedules annual compliance training through VECTOR Solutions LMS [27] that includes modules such as Title XI, Ethics and Protecting Youth.

Title IX

GHC prohibits specific forms of behavior that violate Title IX of the Education Amendments of 1972. GHC will not tolerate sexual misconduct, which is prohibited, and which includes, but is not limited to, domestic violence, dating violence, sexual assault, sexual exploitation, sexual harassment, and stalking. To that end, the college maintains a Title IX program that is designed to ensure university compliance with Title IX. Detailed information about GHC's Title IX process can be found on the college's website [28]. All student Title IX reports are handled by the AVPARR, who serves as the Title IX coordinator. All employee title IX reports are handled by human resources. The specific process and procedure are governed by the Board of Regents Sexual Misconduct Policy [29].

The College has never had any investigations by the U.S. Department of Education's Office of Civil Rights for possible violations alleging sexual violence.

Programs Serving Minors

GHC website states "Georgia Highlands College has established a centralized database to track programs and activities serving minors held on and off campus. Program Administrators must complete the below form and provide 24-hour contact information. Registration also requires certification of compliance with the Policy for Programs and Activities Serving Minors [30]. Prior approval must be attained before events sponsoring minors are held.

Physical Work Environment

An Infographic from the Spring 2023 Annual Organizational Survey [31] and 2023 Presently Attending Student Survey [32] indicates the perception of Safety and Security at GHC for faculty, staff and students; indicating GHC's continued pursuit to provide a safe and secure environment for all its constituents. Of note:

  • 92% of employees note that the campus is well maintained and clean.
  • 97% of employees note a safe environment during the daytime and 91% at night.
  • 85% believe GHC has taken the necessary measure to provide an environment of security and
  • Over 90% of students agree that GHC provides a safe campus.

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the college now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
