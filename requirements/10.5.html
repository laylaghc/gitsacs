Standard 10.5

Standard:

The institution publishes admissions policies consistent with its mission. Recruitment materials and presentations accurately represent the practices, policies, and accreditation status of the institution. The institution also ensures that independent contractors or agents used for recruiting purposes and for admission activities are governed by the same principles and policies as institutional employees. (Admissions policies and practices)

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

Georgia Highlands College (GHC) is a state college in the University System of Georgia (USG). The College operates under the authority of the Board of Regents (BOR). GHC serves as a point of access to higher education in Northwest Georgia. GHC's admissions policies are in keeping with its mission and the policies and procedures set forth by the USG and BOR.

GHC's Access Mission

The mission statement of GHC is as follows:

The mission of Georgia Highlands College, a state college of the University System of Georgia, is to provide access to excellent educational opportunities for the intellectual, cultural, and physical development of a diverse population through pre-baccalaureate associate degree transfer programs, career associate degree programs, and targeted baccalaureate degree programs that meet the economic development needs of the region [1].

In addition to the mission statement, the BOR and USG describe expectations of the mission of a state college. Specifically:

Institutions included in the balanced bachelor's and associate-level state colleges group offer bachelor's degrees, associate programs, and general education courses, but no graduate programs. These state colleges are characterized as balanced bachelor's and associate-level degrees with bachelor's programs focused on specialized academic and economic development program areas and regional, college-educated workforce needs. The Nexus degree can be offered at state colleges. The emphasis at these state colleges is on teaching and service with limited focus on basic or applied research activity (BOR 2.8 [2]).

Admissions Department

Contained under GHC's Unit Mission Statements and Goals, the admissions department has its individual mission statement [3]. It notes:

The mission of the Office of Admissions is to recruit and enroll students by providing the highest level of service and professionalism to students, their families, and the community, ensuring the admissions process will be consistent, fair, and equitable. The Office of Admissions will provide effective and efficient services that support the College mission.

The department has established a set of mission goals [3]. They are:

  1. Ensure that Admissions and Recruitment staff adhere to the highest ethical standards for the profession and that all federal, state, University System of Georgia, and internal policies are followed. [emphasis added]
  2. Provide timely and accurate admission processing services for applications and transcript evaluations.
  3. Deliver timely and accurate communication to improve community outreach, facilitate internal and external information sharing, and enhance recruitment efforts.
  4. Seek to continually improve technology-related functions in the admissions, recruitment, and enrollment processes to ensure services are delivered in an accurate and timely manner.
  5. Maintain, strengthen, and expand collaborative efforts with internal and external stakeholders to build a strong network of support for the institution.

F-1 Student Visa Category:

Once admitted, student must follow the rules and regulations required by the USCIS Customs and Immigration Services in order to maintain an F-1 student visa status.

Language Proficiency. In accordance with the mission, GHC serves a diverse population of students with a range of backgrounds. English Language Proficiency requirements apply to students in all the above categories. Students who are non-native speakers of English or who transfer from an institution of higher education outside the U.S. where English was not the language of instruction are required to show English language proficiency. English Language Proficiency can be satisfied by English transfer credit, four units of college prep English earned at an accredited U.S. high school, or qualifying test scores on the TOEFL, IELTS, SAT, ACT, or Accuplacer Tests. This requirement is published on the How to Apply [4] webpage of the GHC website and in the college catalog [5].

Application. All GHC students begin the admissions process by clicking on the Apply Now link which is visible on the GHC homepage [8], the Future Students website [9], the admissions department webpage [10], and others. This link takes future students to the common application managed by Georgia Futures, an initiative of the Georgia Student Finance Commission.

Students must complete the following steps for application:

  1. Apply ONLINE and pay the $30.00 non-refundable application fee.
  2. Submit all official high school and/or college transcripts directly to GHC.
  3. Submit all official test scores (ACT, SAT, or Next Generation Accuplacer) directly to GHC, if applicable based on admissions category criteria.
  4. Submit Verification of Lawful Presence.
  5. Submit Certificate of Immunization.
  6. Apply for Financial Aid.

Students with Criminal Records. In keeping with the mission of access and opportunity, GHC outlines policy and a procedure for accepting students with criminal records. This procedure is documented in the Policies and Procedures manual [11] and details the admissions process for students with criminal, academic misconduct, or school disciplinary charges or convictions, as self-identified on the GHC admissions application.

Admission to Career Degree, Bachelor's, and Nexus Programs

General admission to the College is not a guarantee for admission to the limited admissions programs. All students can meet the requirements to enter these space-limited programs. Admission policies for each limited admission programs are published in the GHC catalog [5] and on the GHC website. GHC's limited admission programs [12] are as follows:

  • Associate of Science Degree in Nursing, Human Services, and Dental Hygiene
  • Bachelor of Business Administration degree is offered in Healthcare Management and Logistics and Supply Chain Management
  • Bachelor of Science degree is offered in Building Information Modeling Management, Criminal Justice, Dental Hygiene, Health Science, and Nursing
  • Nexus degree is offered in Supply Chain Management in Financial Technology

The requirements for these programs are routinely updated on each program website in accordance with the accrediting agencies of these programs.

Exceptions to GHC's Admission Policies

BOR Policy Manual 4.2.1.3 [13] permits limited exceptions to Freshman admissions requirements as follows:

USG institutions may admit freshman applicants not meeting freshman requirements but showing evidence of college readiness using the following alternative categories.

Limited Admissions

In recognition of the fact that a limited number of applicants do not meet established standards but demonstrate special potential for success, institutions may admit a limited number of applicants under the Limited Admission category.

The Limited Admission category of admission is comprised of a general Limited Admission provision and a Presidential Exception provision. Institutions are restricted by sector to a maximum number of applicants who may be admitted in this category. The number of traditional freshmen who can be granted Limited Admission for the entire academic year will be no more than the following percentages of the institution's annual first-time freshman headcount enrollment. …

Research Universities -- Up to 7 percent
Comprehensive Universities -- Up to 15 percent
State Universities -- Up to 20 percent
State Colleges: Up to 33 percent

Non-traditional freshmen are not included in the Limited Admissions percentage, nor are they included in determining the base.

Limited Admission Provision

Institutions may consider applicants meeting all the following for admission under this provision: …

State Colleges
  • Graduation from high school as outlined in Section 3.1.1.1; or,
  • A state-issued high school equivalency diploma or certificate earned through the successful completion of the GED, TASC or HiSet test. An applicant who presents a state-issued high school diploma or certificate is expected to be at least 18 years of age and for his or her high school class to have graduated; however, institutions may make exceptions on a case-by-case basis. Certificates of attendance or special education diplomas are not acceptable.
Presidential Exceptions

Each institution's President or his or her designee may grant exceptions to the Limited Admission requirements if the applicant shows promise for academic success and has at least a high school diploma or a state-issued high school equivalency diploma or certificate. Presidential Exceptions are included as part of the institution's maximum percentage for Limited Admission. As stated in the GHC 2023-2024 Catalog [5]: page 18-19.

Final acceptance or denial is determined by the GHC Executive Director of Enrollment Management, subject to the applicant's right of appeal or request reconsideration as a presidential exception. A presidential exception for admission to GHC is considered when an applicant does not meet established criteria for admissions to the College but provides evidence of a significant counterbalancing strength demonstrating the potential to succeed in college.

Freshman applicants who do not meet the minimum high school grade point average must submit official SAT or ACT scores (taken within the last seven years) or take the Accuplacer test and achieve scores that can be used as a counterbalancing strength to be re-evaluated for a presidential exception admission decision.

Presidential Exception Provision

Each institution President or his or her designee may, under special and rare circumstances, grant exceptions to the above general Limited Admission provision if the applicant shows promise for academic success. Institutions shall use multiple measures, such as interviews, portfolios, and records of experiential achievements whenever possible.

At least one of the following must be held for consideration under the Presidential Exception provision:

  • A high school diploma from an accredited or approved high school as specified in BOR Policy Section 4.2.1.1 [14] (certificate of attendance or special education diplomas are not acceptable).
  • A state-issued high school equivalency certificate or diploma earned through the successful completion of the GED, TASC, or HiSet test. An applicant who presents a state-issued diploma or certificate is expected to be at least 18 years of age and for his or her high school class to have graduated; however, institutions may make exceptions on a case-by-case basis. Certificates of attendance or special education diplomas are not acceptable.

Applicants admitted under the Presidential Exception provision are included in the number allowed for an institution under the Limited Admission category.

Applicants with Outstanding Scores

Institutions may grant admission to applicants who demonstrate very high academic ability by achieving a Total SAT or Composite ACT score in the upper five percent of national college-bound seniors for the most recent test year and who show other evidence of college readiness.

Admission of Students with Disabilities

Because the core curriculum of each institution requires students to complete college-level courses in English, mathematics, social science, and science, all students must complete the RHSC in these areas. Applicants with disabilities that preclude the acquisition of a foreign language may petition for admission without this requirement according to procedures established by the USG.

The access mission of the USG's State Colleges is upheld here since the percentage limitation on determining the number of Limited Admission acceptances at State Colleges is substantially higher than the percentages for the other three sectors by policy.

In practice, GHC has used limited admission waivers. GHC's [Traditional Freshman Accepted] headcount for Fall 2022 was 1348. The limited admission provision was 17.8% of that number or 240 acceptances. GHC's use of the Presidential Exception provision was also rare. Only 12 applicants were granted admission as Presidential Exceptions for Fall 2022 and only six Presidential Exceptions were granted for Spring 2023.

Example Process: Presidential Exception
  1. Applicants who do not meet minimum admission requirements for their applicant category are denied through normal GHC admissions processes.
  2. Denied applicants receive an email with denial decision, advising them to submit SAT, ACT or Accuplacer test scores to be used as a counterbalancing strength for reconsideration for a presidential exception admission decision
  3. If SAT, ACT, or Accuplacer test scores meet the minimum counterbalancing minimums(s), admissions processors make a first-tier presidential exception admission decision
  4. If SAT, ACT or Accuplacer test scores do not meet minimum counterbalancing minimum(s), first-tier denial remains in place
  5. Applicant has the option to appeal to the President for a second-tier presidential exception interview.
  6. The President will determine if the applicant has a sufficient support system in place, and an actionable plan for achieving academic success. If not, the first-tier denial remains in place. If so, the President sends an email to the Assistant Director of Admissions authorizing a second-tier presidential exception
  7. The second-tier presidential exception email is archived in the students scanned file.

Recruitment Materials and Presentations

GHC continually reviews and updates its recruiting materials to ensure accurate representation of the College's programs, practices, and policies. The College Catalog [5] is the primary source for publishing information about the College's programs, practices, and policies. Annual editions are available in print and online. As programs and policies change mid-year, those changes are published on the applicable webpage of GHC's website and announced in a daily newsletter, GHC Inform. Deans, Chairs, and program directors communicate any changes in policy or practice directly with the recruitment team, the Orientation leaders, and the Advising department through regular communication with the Office of Enrollment Management. Key stakeholders in this area of the College meet monthly as the Enrollment Management Action Team. All members of the institution are invited to monthly online town-hall forums to discuss changes to policy or other key pieces of practices and policies as they relate to recruitment, retention, and graduation efforts.

The Executive Director of Institutional Advancement is responsible for the logistics of training recruiters to establish and maintain school and community contacts, manage event scheduling, and coordinate activities with campus deans, advisors, and individual schools of the College. Additionally, recruiters work closely with Admissions, the Registrar's Office, Financial Aid, and Student Support Services to understand the GHC student onboarding process. Independent Contractors and Agents

GHC uses a communications and recruitment platform called Full Measure. Prior to utilizing the service, GHC performed a cybersecurity assessment of this system using Educause's Higher Education Community Vendor Assessment Toolkit (HECVAT). A report was provided to GHC's Chief Financial Officer and our President upon completion of this review, in accordance with Section 3.4.4 of the USG Business Procedures Manual [16]. This process ensures that Full Measure is governed by the same principles and policies related to student data and engagement as are institutional employees.

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the College now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
