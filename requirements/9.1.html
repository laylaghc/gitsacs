Standard 9.1

Standard:

Educational programs (a) embody a coherent course of study, (b) are compatible with the stated mission and goals, and (c) are based upon fields of study appropriate to higher education. (Program content)

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

Georgia Highlands College's (GHC) educational programs embody a coherent course of study, are compatible with the stated mission and goals, and are based upon fields of study appropriate to higher education.

Degree programs are defined and outlined by the University System of Georgia (USG). As detailed in the Institutional Summary [1] of this report, GHC offers 14 educational programs that fall within four categories: two transfer associate degrees, three career associate degrees, one Nexus associate degree, and eight baccalaureate degrees. The Nexus degree is a specially named associate degree, as established by our governing board [2]. As GHC is a member of the University System of Georgia (USG), all educational programs at GHC are subject to the various policies and procedures outlined in the USG's Board of Regents (BOR) Policy Manual [3], Academic & Student Affairs Handbook [4], and other appropriate sources. As per USG Policy (USG Academic Affairs & Student Handbook, Section 2.3.2 [5]), all of GHC's educational programs are approved by the USG. Coherent Courses of Study

All Georgia Highlands College (GHC) degree programs contain a coherent course of study that requires students to build a core academic content base of knowledge before progressing through an individual program pathway. Every credit program at Georgia Highlands College embodies a coherent course of study by offering and publicizing a semester-by-semester sequence for all program offerings via program checklists [6]. Further, each program is designed either to provide job-related skills or to meet the general education requirements outlined in the BOR Policy Manual 3.3.1 [7].

Core Curriculum

The core curriculum specifies that each USG institution's core curriculum consists of 42 semester credit hours spread across various designated areas. The USG's Council on General Education reviews/approves the learning outcomes for each area of the core curriculum for all USG institutions (BOR Policy 3.3.1 [7]). All courses that are to be used in Areas A-E are reviewed/approved by the relevant USG Academic Advisory Committee (USG Academic & Student Affairs Handbook, Section 2.4.6 [8]; USG Academic Affairs Division Regents' Academic Advisory Committees [9]). This rigorous review process helps ensure that each USG institution has a coherent core curriculum.

GHC's core curriculum has been subject to this specified USG review/approval process, which helps ensure its coherence. As is described in the GHC's Catalog [10], the forty-two credits of general education courses that make up GHC core curriculum are divided among the following areas:

  • Area A: Essential Skills – 9 hours
  • Area B: Institutional Options – 4-5 hours
  • Area C: Humanities/Fine Arts – 6 hours
  • Area D: Science, Mathematics, & Technology – 10-11 hours
  • Area E: Social Sciences – 12 hours

Additionally, GHC students also complete 18 hours of coursework in Area F. Area F consists of lower-division courses that serve as preparation and prerequisites for specific upper-division major courses in baccalaureate programs. This, again, is consistent with USG policy (USG Academic & Student Affairs Handbook Section 2.4.2 [11]).

Educational Programs at GHC

The academic coursework contained in all GHC program pathways is designed to advance the students' skill levels through a sequence of increasingly complex coursework, ultimately leading to degree completion. This progression ensures that students are doing more than just achieving content mastery or accumulating credits. Students are also acquiring the necessary skills, such as critical thinking, communication, analytics, collaboration, and technology expertise, needed to succeed in a diverse and global society. This approach to the process of learning is compatible with the GHC mission and ensures the academic rigor of coursework required to successfully complete each individual pathway of study is appropriate to higher education.

Information about all of GHC's educational programs are available online [12], as well as in the GHC Catalog [10] which is published annually.

Transfer Associate Degree Programs. Transfer degrees offered at Georgia Highlands College lead to an Associate of Arts (AA) or an Associate of Science (AS) degree. These programs include the core curriculum designed to provide the foundation for completing the programs of study at four-year colleges and universities and to facilitate the transfer of credit among the schools within the University System of Georgia.

GHC's AA and AS degrees conform to the BOR policy on transfer degrees (BOR 3.3.1 [7] and 3.8.2.1 [13]). BOR policy 3.8.2.1 states:

Associate of Arts (A.A.) and Associate of Science (A.S.) degrees are primarily intended to be transfer degrees leading to the baccalaureate degree, although some provide students with a recognized two-year degree credential and employment opportunities upon graduation. All A.A. and A.S. degrees shall consist of 60 semester credit hours; 42 hours of the required core curriculum coursework as outlined in Board Policy, 18 hours of lower division requirements related to a bachelor's degree field of study (Area F), and do not include institutional requirements in physical education, activity, basic health, or orientation. Associate degrees beyond 60 credit hours require the approval of the USG chief academic officer.

GHC AA or AS degree graduates are required to complete a minimum number of core curriculum components in Areas A-E as follows:

  • Area A: Essential Skills (9 hours)
  • Area B: Institutional Options (4-5 hours)
  • Area C: Humanities/Fine Arts (6 hours)
  • Area D: Science, Mathematics and Technology (10-11 hours)
  • Area E: Social Sciences (12 hours)
  • Area F: Lower-Division Requirements (18 hours)

Transfer associate degrees include Area F courses, which are lower-division level courses numbered in the 1000-to-2000 range related to a particular transfer pathway.

Program checklists for the various associate degree pathways can be found on the College website [6].

Career Associate Degree Programs. The BOR allows for a reduced number of general education courses for associate degrees that are career-focused programs designed to prepare students for immediate employment upon graduation. GHC requires 38 hours of general academic courses for its Dental Hygiene Associate of Science degree, which accounts for 50 percent of the 77 hours required. GHC requires a minimum of 25 hours of general education courses for its Associate of Science in Human Services degree, which accounts for 38 percent of the 66 hours required to graduate. GHC requires a minimum of 38 hours of general academic courses for its Associate of Science in Nursing, which accounts for 51 percent of the 74 hours required to graduate. The core curriculum for GHC career focused programs can be found on the College website [14] under career programs and in the GHC Catalog [10].

Nexus Associate Degree Program. The Nexus degree began in 2020 and requires the same 60 credit-hour completion as an associate degree (USG Nexus [2]). GHC's sole Nexus degree offered at this time is targeted toward the high demand career area in FinTech with Logistics and Supply Chain Management components. Course requirements are defined by BOR policy 3.8.3 [18]. The Nexus degree requires 42 credit-hours of general education core (Area A-F), 18 credit hours of coursework focused on skills and knowledge required of a major industry or field, and six credit hours of substantive experiential learning. Compatibility with GHC Mission and Goals

The educational programs at (GHC) are directly related and appropriate to the mission and goals of the institution and degrees awarded. The mission of GHC is as follows:

The mission of Georgia Highlands College, a state college of the University System of Georgia, is to provide access to excellent educational opportunities for the intellectual, cultural, and physical development of a diverse population through pre-baccalaureate associate degree transfer programs, career associate degree programs, and targeted baccalaureate degree programs that meet the economic development needs of the region (GHC Website [14]).

This mission corresponds with the institutional mission of an Associate Dominant-Select Baccalaureate State College as defined in Policy 2.8 [29] of the Board of Regents of the University System of Georgia. This mission includes "offer bachelor's degrees, associate programs, and general education courses, but no graduate programs. These state colleges are characterized as balanced bachelor's and associate-level degrees with bachelor's programs focused on specialized academic and economic development program areas and regional, college-educated workforce needs."

The 14 educational programs are limited to associate degrees and a few professionally oriented baccalaureate degrees. All degrees are designed to "meet the economic development needs of the region." New programs proposed by the institution must be approved by the Chancellor of the University System (BOR 3.6.1 [30]). Approval requires the institution to complete a USG Academic Degree Program Application [31], which must include the ways the program is aligned "with local, regional, and/or state talent demand or workforce strategies." Additionally, existing programs must undergo a Comprehensive Program Review every seven years (BOR 2.3.6 [32]). Such a review includes an analysis of "quality, viability, and productivity … as appropriate to the institution's mission." For GHC, that mission is focused on access to educational opportunities and meeting the economic development of our service area.

Fields of Study Appropriate to Higher Education

The consideration and reviews given new program proposals at both the institutional level and at the BOR ensure appropriateness and coherence for GHC's degree programs (Completed Degree Proposal for B.S. in Environmental and Natural Resources [33]). The USG Program Review process (BOR 3.6 [34]) outlines this procedure. GHC follows USG procedure to solicit comprehensive input from the college's faculty and administration on any proposed field of study prior to submission to USG. The USG further ensures the appropriateness of fields of study to higher education and to GHC based on its mission as an access institution. This system-wide perspective of the USG involves review, approval, and oversight of the appropriateness of higher education programs at 26 unique public colleges and universities in Georgia.

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the college now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
