Standard 6.1

Standard:

The institution employs an adequate number of full-time faculty members to support the mission and goals of the institution. (Full-time faculty)  [CR]

Compliance Non-Compliance Not Applicable

Narrative:

Georgia Highlands College (GHC), a state college in the University System of Georgia (USG) and under the governance of the Board of Regents (BOR) employs an adequate number of full-time faculty members to support the mission and goals of the institution. Faculty members at GHC perform essential roles which enable the College to accomplish its mission of providing "access to excellent educational opportunities for the intellectual, cultural, and physical development of a diverse population..."

GHC's Mission Statement

The mission statement of GHC is as follows:

The mission of Georgia Highlands College, a state college of the University System of Georgia, is to provide access to excellent educational opportunities for the intellectual, cultural, and physical development of a diverse population through pre-baccalaureate associate degree transfer programs, career associate degree programs, and targeted baccalaureate degree programs that meet the economic development needs of the region [1].

In addition to the College's mission statement, the BOR and USG define their expectations of the mission of a state college. Specifically:

Institutions included in the balanced bachelor's and associate-level state colleges group offer bachelor's degrees, associate degree programs, and general education courses, but no graduate programs. These state colleges are characterized as balanced bachelor's and associate-level degrees with bachelor's programs focused on specialized academic and economic development program areas and regional, college-educated workforce needs. The Nexus degree can be offered at state colleges. The emphasis at these state colleges is on teaching and service with limited focus on basic or applied research activity (BOR 2.8 [2]).

Definition of Full-Time Faculty

The BOR and USG broadly define membership in the faculty as the "Corps of Instruction" at all colleges in the university system by policy. BOR 3.2.1.1 [3] states:

Full-time professors, associate professors, assistant professors, instructors, lecturers, senior lecturers, principal lecturers and teaching personnel with such other titles, as may be approved by the Board, shall be the Corps of Instruction. Full-time research and extension personnel and duly certified librarians will be included in the Corps of Instruction on the basis of comparable training. Persons holding part-time and adjunct appointments or other honorary titles shall not be considered to be members of the faculty.

GHC employs full-time faculty at the instructor, assistant professor, associate professor, and full professor ranks. The following table details the rank of the 135 full-time faculty members employed by GHC.

As an institution, GHC is particularly focused on "providing access to excellent educational opportunities" and expects faculty to engage in teaching, service, and scholarship/professional development. Through the common expectations of faculty [5], GHC details the general requirements for all groups of faculty at the college and the specific details for teaching, service, and scholarship/professional development. Specific versions of this document are available for teaching [6] and library [7] faculty.

The teaching faculty member position description [8] notes that the "primary responsibilities [for teaching faculty] are preparing for and delivering excellent instruction, providing academic advising, and contributing to the college through ongoing service." Of note, GHC teaching faculty are expected to teach 30 credit hours or equivalent per year as assigned and are not expected to engage in faculty-lead research in the traditional sense. Instead, they engage in professional development and scholarship of teaching and learning, as expressed by the Boyer Model. This is consistent with the mission of the College and systemwide expectations of the mission of a state college. Collectively, faculty members at GHC support the College's mission in the areas of teaching excellence, curriculum development/oversight, identification and assessment of appropriate student learning outcomes, advising, and professional development.

Definition of Part-Time (Adjunct) Faculty

GHC employs part-time faculty members who are properly credentialed and teach courses on an as-needed, limited-term basis (semesterly) at the discretion of the institution. Part-time faculty members do not accrue time toward tenure and are not eligible for USG benefits. They work 19 or less hours per week and sign an agreement for each appointment, in lieu of a contract.

GHC's Organizational Structure of Academic Functions

To support the mission, GHC has created the Division of Academic Affairs, which is led by the Provost and Chief Academic Officer (CAO). The Provost and CAO is responsible for the overall management of academic affairs and supervision of the Academic Deans as demonstrated by the Provost position description [9]. The academic units of academic affairs consist of the School of Business and Professional Studies, Atrium Health Floyd School of Health Sciences, School of Humanities, School of Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics, and School of Social Sciences and Education. Each of these areas is overseen by an Academic Dean who is a credentialed faculty member in each school and supervises its overall academic functions as documented in their position descriptions [10]. With their professional judgment as a guide and informed by accepted practices within the university system, Academic Deans work with the Provost and CAO to determine the number of full-time faculty needed to support the College's mission and desired pragmatic outcomes. Together they consider program content, specialization, safety, and enrollment in the specific subject. Consideration is always given to the availability of funding and qualified faculty already employed by the College. Academic Deans are assisted by one or more Division Chairs, who conduct teaching observations, evaluate faculty, schedule courses, and hire part-time faculty as the Provost's Office organizational chart [11] and chair position description [12] demonstrates.

Determining the Number of Adequate Faculty. With their professional judgment as a guide and informed by accepted practices within the university system, Academic Deans work with the Provost and CAO to determine the number of full-time faculty needed to support the College's mission and desired program outcomes. Together they consider program content, specialization, safety, and enrollment in the specific subject. Consideration is always given to the availability of funding and qualified faculty already employed by the College. GHC has set the minimum threshold for student credit hours produced by full-time faculty in each academic school to 50%.

GHC Student-to-Faculty Ratios

The University System of Georgia is a large and robust system with 26 institutions of higher education. USG considers nine institutions to be the "state college sector." These institutions are very similar to GHC in that they have an access mission and offer associate degrees and a limited number of career-focused baccalaureate degrees. None of the colleges in this sector offer graduate degrees. Therefore, these institutions offer a comparable peer group.

Georgia Highlands College is a multi-campus, state college member of the University System of Georgia. Founded in 1970 as Floyd Junior College, the College now serves thousands of students from over 30 counties in Northwest Georgia. GHC has five sites in Rome, Cartersville, Marietta, and Dallas, as well as a robust online program. GHC offers over 40 areas of study with associate degree and bachelor's degree options both in the classroom and online. GHC is proud to support the local economy with a regional economic impact of nearly $170 million.
